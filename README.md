# Mirkwood
> *To see is to be bound.*

**A synthetic data demonstration of cross-channel correlation as a reconstruction methodology for passively observable emissions.**

---

### What this is

Operators conducting surveillance across multiple channels (trunked voice, BLE, plate readers) rely on per-channel pseudonymity to maintain deniability. They use rotating talkgroups, randomized MAC addresses, and handle systems. The entire model assumes activity on one channel is not jointly observable with activity on another.

That assumption is mathematically and architecturally false.

A passive observer capturing multiple channels simultaneously collapses pseudonyms across channels through temporal and spatial correlation alone. No cryptography is broken. No payloads are decrypted. No cooperative infrastructure is queried. The joint distribution simply does not lie the way individual channels do.

Each channel is individually deniable; the joint distribution is not. The operators with the most to lose from this reality are the least likely to publish about it.

Mirkwood is a research instrument. It is not a platform, a product, or an operational tool. It exists to make a reconstruction methodology legible to those who require it: journalists, civil rights litigators, public defenders, oversight researchers, and anyone tasked with establishing patterns of practice designed to remain unprovable.

### The envelope

Mirkwood operates exclusively on synthetic data. No real RF, telemetry, or captures are ingested, stored, or displayed at any point.

The synthetic corpus is generated by a scenario engine modeled on the documented characteristics of real-world environments. Every identifier, location, timestamp, and event is fabricated.

This boundary is load-bearing. It is the absolute condition under which this research demonstrates a methodology without prejudicing lawfully collected evidence, FOIA releases, ongoing investigations, or individual privacy. Real-world application of this methodology belongs entirely to those holding the lawful predicate to apply it, governed by their respective standards.

This is not a temporary convenience. It is the permanent operating envelope.

### What the demo shows

Three synthetic channels are ingested into a unified spatiotemporal event store under a common schema. A natural-language analyst interface translates intent into structured queries against this fused corpus.

Queries take the form:
> *"Show all short-range device hashes that maintained ±80m proximity to logical ID 0x3A9F during its last 5 tactical transitions."*

These resolve to correlation locks in real-time against the synthetic scenario.

The natural language interface is not a demo gimmick; it is the core argument. A threat whose practical barrier is "knowing the right SQL" possesses a fundamentally different risk profile from a threat whose practical barrier is "typing the question in English." The latter describes the current reality. Pretending otherwise perpetuates a broken threat model.

### What is not in here

This repository does not, and will not, provide materials sufficient to reproduce the methodology against real data. Specifically withheld:

* Ingestion adapters for any real protocol.
* Schema definitions beyond the requirements of the synthetic scenario.
* Correlation thresholds, windowing logic, and estimator tuning.
* Scenario generator calibration data.
* Configuration presets, deployment guides, edge node hardware specifications, and build instructions.

This withholding is structural. The methodology is already available to motivated adversaries; the accountability side requires it to be legible. Distributing a turnkey reconstruction tool serves the adversary. Requests for withheld components will be ignored.

### The accessibility point

Sub-$900 of commercial-off-the-shelf hardware is sufficient to stand up a node capable of this correlation. That is not a specification; it is proof of viability. The threat is not exotic. The threat model is obsolete. This research closes that gap for the people who need to know.

### Methodology for accountability use

The generalizable output offered to the accountability community is the framing itself: treat ambient emissions as a joint distribution, not as independent channels. The rest is engineering.

This framing is productive for practitioners already holding the lawful predicate in the following contexts:
* Reconstructing activity patterns from FOIA-released records spanning multiple modalities.
* Pattern-of-practice analysis in civil rights litigation where individual incidents are disputed but the aggregate pattern forms the claim.
* Corroborating testimony against independently established telemetry.
* Independent review of official incident reconstructions where underlying data is available under lawful process.

In these cases, the framing is the capability. The tool is whatever the practitioner already lawfully accesses. Mirkwood does not need to be in the loop for the methodology to apply, and it will not be.

### Ethics

Countersurveillance forensics possesses an unavoidable dual-use property. The methodology allowing the accountability side to reconstruct an operator's activity is the exact methodology the operator uses against targets. It is the same math and the same pipeline.

Publishing on one side of this dynamic and not the other is justified by the power asymmetry. Institutional operators possess resources, legal cover, and secrecy. The people subject to their attention possess, at best, ambient evidence and a legal system requiring established patterns of practice to act. Research making the reconstruction side legible—under a synthetic data envelope, without a reproduction path—corrects this asymmetry. Shipping a turnkey reconstruction tool does not.

This calculation is contestable, and principled disagreement is welcome. Contestable does not mean wrong; it is the necessary condition for honest work in this space.
