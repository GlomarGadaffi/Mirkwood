The Synthetic Generation Environment (The Diorama)
To generate a mathematically valid, multi-modal synthetic corpus without leaking emissions into the public spectrum, the adversary must be simulated physically. The Mirkwood synthetic data is not generated by a script; it is scraped from a physical diorama of constrained RF.

Here is the architectural shape of the generation environment, stripped of the exact model numbers and frequencies that would render it immediately reproducible.

1. The RF Emitters (The "Tactical Unit")
To generate valid Phase II frames and control-channel transitions without violating spectrum regulations, the transmission physics must be choked.

The Transceivers: Decommissioned law enforcement surplus portables operating on the relevant 700/800 MHz bands.

The RF Constraint: 50-Ohm SMA/BNC RF dummy loads. By replacing the antennas with dummy loads, the transmission power is absorbed and converted to marginal heat, collapsing the effective transmission radius from miles to inches. The radios interact, the protocol behaves normally, but the emissions never leave the workbench.

2. The Commercial IoT Targets (The "Telemetry Exhaust")
To simulate the proximity beacons of modern fleet vehicles and body-worn camera systems, the environment relies on standard MAC spoofing.

The Beacons: Generic, commercial-off-the-shelf microcontroller development boards capable of Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) transmission.

The Firmware: Scripts designed to continuously broadcast BLE advertisement packets with hardcoded Organizationally Unique Identifiers (OUIs). By assigning the specific IEEE OUIs registered to major public safety and surveillance vendors, a $5 microcontroller becomes functionally identical to a proprietary tactical wearable from the perspective of a passive sniffer.

3. The Ground Truth Data (The Validation Layer)
To prove the correlation engine can resolve these physical events against historical data, Mirkwood queries a local, air-gapped cache.

The Dataset: Offline BLE geolocation references and standard IEEE MAC assignment tables.

The Physics of the Lab
The aggregate cost to construct this closed-loop generation environment is under $1,500.
